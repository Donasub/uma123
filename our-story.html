"We started this because we were tired of paying ₦12,000 for a t-shirt shipped from Atlanta — with our own university's crest on it. Spelled wrong."

Universe Merch Africa is an officially licensed campus apparel platform designed, printed, and fulfilled out of Lagos. We partner directly with schools so students can rep their campuses without getting ripped off.

The Origin

Lagos, 2023. Two friends at a UI homecoming, trying to buy a hoodie. The only options: a stall selling fakes printed on t-shirt blanks that had clearly been through a war, or a Shopify store shipping from Atlanta for a six-week lead time and a $40 price tag. We bought neither.

Then we asked around. Every single person we knew — whether UNILAG, Ibadan, OAU, Covenant, ABU — had the same frustration. Their school had a beautiful crest, a legitimate brand, a hundred thousand students ready to represent, and nowhere to buy proper merchandise. Meanwhile the schools themselves were losing revenue to third parties printing knockoffs.

"What if the schools controlled the brand, the students got the quality, and everyone kept the money on the continent?"

So we built it. We started with three universities — UI, UNILAG, OAU — and one rule: nothing ships unless the school's brand team signs off on it. Every crest is licensed. Every design is approved. Every royalty goes back to the institution.

That was early 2024. Today we're in 40+ universities and 80+ secondary schools across Nigeria, Ghana, Kenya, and South Africa, with a dedicated print facility in Ikeja, Lagos, and a team of 34 designers, operators, and campus reps who — almost to a person — are alumni of the schools they serve.

We're still just getting started.

The Road So Far

Three years,
forty campuses,
zero knockoffs.

2023Q3

A conversation at a homecoming

Co-founders Ayo and Kemi realize every African university has a huge brand problem: beautiful crests, no authorized merchandise, millions in revenue flowing to unlicensed sellers overseas.

2024Q1

First licensing deals signed

Universities of Ibadan, Lagos, and Obafemi Awolowo become Universe Merch's founding partners. First drop — the OG Varsity Hoodie — sells out in 72 hours.

2024Q3

Lagos print facility goes live

2,400 sq ft in Ikeja with DTG printers, embroidery machines, and a pattern room. No more outsourcing. No more delays. Everything under one roof.

2025Q2

Expansion across West Africa

University of Ghana, KNUST, and University of Cape Coast come on. First Ghana-based campus rep hired. Cross-border fulfillment via partners in Accra.

2025Q4

Secondary schools launch

Old-student associations become the most-requested customer segment. We launch a dedicated high-school division with King's College, Queen's College, and Mayflower as flagship partners.

2026Today

40+ schools and counting

Over 200,000 pieces shipped. ₦480M in royalties returned to partner schools. Active presence in four countries. Still no knockoffs. Still made in Lagos.

What We Stand For

Six principles.
No exceptions.

Everyone says they have values. These are the ones we actually argue about in meetings, hire against, and fire for.

01

Licensing is non-negotiable

If the school hasn't signed off, we don't print it. No "gray market" stock. No "limited test runs" without permission. Ever.

02

Made here

Every shirt, hoodie, and cap is printed in Ikeja. We're not a dropshipper. We're a manufacturer. Jobs stay on the continent.

03

Students come first

Every product is priced so a Nigerian student can actually afford it. Margins are thinner because the mission matters more.

04

Design like we went there

Because we mostly did. Every school design is led by an alum of that school. Campus nicknames get honored. Inside jokes get referenced.

05

Royalties always

Every partner school earns a share on every item sold. Transparent reporting, monthly payouts, no backroom deals.

06

Build for the long haul

We're not chasing a quick exit. We're building the merchandising layer for African education — a 20-year project, not a 2-year sprint.
