All our pieces are cut with African body shapes in mind — roomier through the chest and shoulders, standard through the length. Pick your product below, grab a tape, and let's get this right.

How to measure

The tee method.

Grab a tee you already like. Lay it flat on a table. Measure from seam to seam — don't stretch the fabric.

01
Chest — 2.5 cm below the armpit, pit to pit across the front.
02
Length — top of shoulder (at the neck seam) down to the hem.
03
Shoulder — seam to seam across the top of the back.

How to measure

The hoodie method.

Same flat-lay method as a tee, but pay closer attention to sleeve length — hoodies run longer.

01
Chest — pit to pit across the front, flat.
02
Length — neck seam at the back down to the hem ribbing.
03
Sleeve — shoulder seam to the end of the cuff, following the curve.

How to measure

The varsity method.

Varsity jackets are structured, so measure your body directly — not a garment. Stand relaxed, arms down.

01
Chest — wrap the tape around the fullest part, keep it level.
02
Shoulder — bony point to bony point, across the back.
03
Sleeve — shoulder bone → elbow → wrist, arm slightly bent.

Quick reference

No tape? No wahala.

Use a piece of string. Wrap, mark, then measure the string against a ruler.

01
Above ears — about 1 cm / half an inch above the ear tops.
02
Across forehead — over the widest part, not too tight.
03
Round up — if you're between sizes, the bigger one will feel better all day.
Before you order

Three things we always tell people.

01 · Between sizes?

Size down for a fitted look

If you're on the fence, our pieces are cut roomy. Going down one size gives you the "just right" fit. Going up gives you oversized/streetwear.

02 · Shrinkage

Cold wash, tumble low

We pre-shrink everything, but you can expect 1–3% shrinkage after first wash. Cold wash + low tumble keeps the fit honest for years.

03 · Still not sure?

Order two, return one

Order both sizes with free returns on one. Whichever fits wins. No guilt, no restocking fees — just don't remove the tags.
